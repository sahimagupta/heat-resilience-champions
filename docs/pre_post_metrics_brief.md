# Pre/Post Metrics Brief — Heat Resilience Champions

## Why You Need Both PRE and POST Metrics

**PRE metrics** establish the problem's scale and urgency — they're your pitch. They answer: *"Why should anyone care?"*

**POST metrics** prove your programme worked — they're your proof. They answer: *"Did it actually make a difference?"*

Without PRE: you can't show what changed.
Without POST: you can't prove you caused the change.

**Together**: PRE → Intervention → POST = causal story that funders, governments, and communities trust.

---

## PRE Metrics: The 5 Mandatory Pitch Numbers

Every heat resilience pitch must include these five baseline metrics. They establish the crisis.

| # | Metric | Current Value | Source |
|---|--------|---------------|--------|
| 1 | **Annual heat deaths (global)** | 546,000 | Lancet Countdown 2025 |
| 2 | **Productivity losses** | $1.09 trillion/year | ILO 2024 |
| 3 | **Workers exposed** | 2.41 billion (70% of workforce) | ILO 2024 |
| 4 | **Children affected** | 200M in extreme heat zones; 242M education disrupted | UNICEF 2024 |
| 5 | **Proven reduction potential** | 80% mortality reduction with HAPs | Lancet Europe 2024 |

### Why These Five?

- **Deaths** = human urgency (moral case)
- **Productivity** = economic urgency (business case)
- **Workers** = scale of exposure (policy case)
- **Children** = future generation urgency (development case)
- **80% reduction** = hope (solution case)

## POST Metrics: 3 Tiers of Evidence

### Tier 1 — Immediate Outputs (measure during/right after programme)
*"What did we deliver?"*

| Metric | How to Measure | When |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Champions trained & deployed | Training records + deployment verification | End of training cycle |
| Cool roof m² installed | GPS-tagged records + photos | Ongoing |
| People reached with heat safety info | Campaign analytics + attendance logs | End of outreach |
| Community meetings held | Meeting logs | Monthly |
| Schools with heat protocols | Documentation audit | End of school programme |

### Tier 2 — Short-Term Outcomes (3–12 months)
*"Did behaviour and conditions change?"*

| Metric | How to Measure | When |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Knowledge score improvement | Pre/post standardised test | 3–6 months |
| Indoor temperature reduction (cool roofs) | Temperature loggers | 1–6 months post-install |
| Household heat safety plan adoption | Follow-up survey | 6 months |
| Heat alert response time | Alert logs + community timestamps | Each heat event |
| Energy savings (cool roofs) | Bill comparison | 6 months post-install |

### Tier 3 — Long-Term Impact (1–5 years)
*"Did we save lives and build resilience?"*

| Metric | How to Measure | When |
|--------|---------------|------|
| Heat-related deaths in target area | Hospital/morgue records + verbal autopsy | Annually |
| Heat-related hospital admissions | Health facility data | Annually |
| Champion retention rate | Active status tracking | 12 months+ |
| CO₂ avoided (cool roofs) | Calculated from energy data | Annually |
| School days lost to heat | School attendance records | Annually |
| Economic productivity retained | Labour hour tracking / employer surveys | Annually |

## The Sanity Check: 5 Tests Before You Report

Before publishing any impact number, run these five tests:

### Test 1: Source Check
> *"Can I trace this number to a named, dated, peer-reviewed or institutional source?"*
- ✅ "546K deaths (Lancet Countdown 2025)" → passes
- ❌ "Millions die from heat every year" → fails

### Test 2: Attribution Check
> *"Am I claiming credit only for what my programme caused?"*
- ✅ "Indoor temp dropped 3°C in homes where we installed cool roofs" → passes
- ❌ "Heat deaths dropped 50% in the city" (when your programme covered 2 neighbourhoods) → fails

### Test 3: Denominator Check
> *"Is my percentage based on the right total?"*
- ✅ "70% of our trained Champions deployed" (325 of 500) → passes
- ❌ "We reached 70% of people" (without specifying 70% of what population) → fails

### Test 4: Timeframe Check
> *"Am I comparing the same time periods?"*
- ✅ "Hospital admissions: 340 (2023 pre-programme) vs 280 (2024 post-programme), same heat months" → passes
- ❌ "Admissions dropped from 340 to 280" (comparing peak month to off-season) → fails

### Test 5: Undercount Check
> *"Am I honest about what I can't measure?"*
- ✅ "India officially reports ~110 heat deaths, but Lancet estimates actual figure is 10–100x higher" → passes
- ❌ Reporting official figures without noting known undercount → fails
