# Research Questions Bank — Heat Resilience Champions

**47 Questions · 9 Sectors · Source-Referenced**

---

## 1. Mortality (6 questions)

1. The Lancet Countdown 2025 reports 546,000 heat-related deaths annually — a 167% increase since 2000. What share of this increase is attributable to climate change vs. ageing populations and urbanisation? *(S-01: Lancet Countdown 2025)*

2. Europe recorded 62,000 heat deaths in 2022 and 47,690 in 2023. Does this decline reflect better adaptation or a less severe heat season? *(S-02: Ballester et al. 2023; S-03: Lancet Europe 2024)*

3. India officially reported ~110 heat deaths in 2023 while Ahmedabad's HAP alone averts 1,190/year. What methodological changes would close this undercount gap? *(S-17: NRDC/Ahmedabad)*

4. The Hajj 2024 disaster killed 1,301 pilgrims in temperatures exceeding 51.8°C. What systemic failures allowed mass casualties at a predictable, recurring event? *(S-01: Lancet / AP reporting)*

5. Phoenix (Maricopa County) recorded 602 heat deaths in 2024 despite $100M in cool corridor investment. What populations are current interventions failing to protect? *(S-18: Maricopa County Public Health)*

6. Lancet Europe 2024 shows 80% reduction in heat mortality is achievable with comprehensive HAPs. Why have fewer than 25% of heat-vulnerable cities adopted one? *(S-03: Lancet Europe 2024)*

## 2. Economy (5 questions)

7. ILO estimates $1.09 trillion in annual heat-related productivity losses. How is this distributed across sectors, and which sector has the highest loss-per-worker? *(S-07: ILO 2024)*

8. The projected $4.2 trillion loss by 2030 assumes current trajectory. What intervention portfolio would have the highest ROI in reducing this figure? *(S-08: ILO 2019)*

9. South Asia faces 5.6% GDP loss from heat by 2030 vs. a global average of 2.6%. What makes South Asian economies disproportionately vulnerable? *(S-10: World Bank 2024)*

10. India alone lost 34 million working days to heat in 2023. How do these losses compare to other climate impacts (flooding, drought) in the same year? *(S-07: ILO 2024 / RBI)*

11. The IEA projects 50% growth in cooling energy demand by 2030. Could cool roofs and passive cooling offset this, and at what scale? *(S-24: IEA 2023)*

## 3. Urban & Cities (6 questions)

12. Ahmedabad's HAP costs $0.50 per person per year and averts 1,190 deaths. What is the cost-per-DALY (disability-adjusted life year) saved, and how does it compare to other public health interventions? *(S-17: NRDC 2023)*

13. Barcelona operates 368 cooling shelters by repurposing public spaces. What utilisation rate would justify new-build shelters instead? *(S-19: Barcelona 2024)*

14. Medellín's green corridors achieved 2°C cooling at $6.50/person. How does this scale in cities without existing green space for corridor conversion? *(S-20: C40/Medellín 2023)*

15. Phoenix's urban heat island adds 5–7°C at night. Since nighttime heat is the primary killer, what interventions specifically target nocturnal UHI? *(S-18: Maricopa County)*

16. Ziter et al. found 10% tree canopy increase yields 1.2°C cooling. What's the threshold below which canopy additions have negligible thermal benefit? *(S-22: Ziter/PNAS 2019)*

17. Cities exceeding 35°C for 30+ days will rise to 970 by 2050. Which cities currently below this threshold should be pre-investing in adaptation? *(S-06: Zhao et al. / C40)*

## 4. Children & Schools (5 questions)

18. UNICEF reports 200 million children in extreme heat zones and 242 million with disrupted education. What is the overlap between these populations? *(S-13: UNICEF 2024)*

19. Bangladesh closed 33,000 schools affecting 18 million children in 2024. What is the cumulative learning loss, and does it compound with existing post-COVID deficits? *(S-14: UNICEF/UNESCO 2024)*

20. Park et al. found 21% learning score decline with heat exposure. At what temperature threshold does cognitive decline begin, and is it linear? *(S-15: Park et al. 2020)*

21. 84% of schools in the Global South lack cooling infrastructure. What is the most cost-effective first intervention — cool roofs, fans, vegetation, or schedule shifts? *(S-14: UNESCO 2024)*

22. 559 million children walk to school in extreme heat. What is the health burden of heat exposure during transit vs. during class hours? *(S-13: UNICEF 2024)*

25. Agriculture accounts for 58.4% of heat-exposed workers (1.41 billion). How do smallholder farmers adapt differently from plantation workers? *(S-07: ILO 2024)*

26. Productivity declines 25% above 33°C WBGT. Is this threshold the same for acclimatised workers in tropical climates? *(S-11: Kjellstrom et al. 2018)*

27. 1.6 billion informal workers have no access to formal safety nets. What community-based alternatives could a Champions programme offer? *(S-07: ILO 2024)*

28. Heat stroke has a 50% fatality rate when untreated. What first-response training would have the highest impact if delivered through Champions? *(S-12: WHO)*

## 6. Regional Disparities (5 questions)

## 7. Solutions & Interventions (6 questions)

34. Cool roofs reduce indoor temperature by 2–5°C. What is the performance difference between professional reflective coatings vs. community-applied lime wash? *(S-21: Akbari et al. / S-17: NRDC)*

35. WMO finds 50% mortality reduction with 24–48h early warnings. What is the optimal warning lead time — too early triggers fatigue, too late misses preparation? *(S-23: WMO 2024)*

36. Ahmedabad's HAP model has been replicated to 23 Indian states. What are the failure modes in states where replication didn't produce results? *(S-17: NRDC)*

37. Permeable pavements reduce surface temperature by 5–7°C (LA CoolSeal data). What is the maintenance burden and lifecycle cost compared to traditional surfacing? *(S-21: Berkeley Lab)*

38. Barcelona's 368 climate refuges had 250K visits. What percentage of the vulnerable population does this represent, and who doesn't come? *(S-19: Barcelona 2024)*

39. Lancet Europe 2024 shows 80% mortality reduction is achievable. What is the minimum package of interventions needed to reach 50%? *(S-03: Lancet Europe 2024)*

## 8. Undercounting & Data Gaps (4 questions)

40. India's official heat death count is ~110 while modelling suggests 10–100x more. What are the specific attribution barriers (death certificate coding, hospital access, cultural factors)? *(S-17: NRDC / Lancet)*

41. CDC reports ~2,300 US heat deaths, but studies suggest 5,000–12,000. How should indirect heat deaths (cardiac, renal) be captured? *(S-18: CDC WONDER)*

42. 67% of countries lack enforceable maximum workplace temperature laws. How does policy absence create a data void — no law means no violation means no recording? *(S-07: ILO 2024)*

43. Asia's 20.74% mortality share seems low relative to population and heat exposure. Is the Lancet methodology underweighting Asian excess mortality? *(S-01: Lancet 2025)*
