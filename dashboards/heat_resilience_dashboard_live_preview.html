Why this matters
The institutional momentum behind heat resilience is stronger than it has ever been. In November 2025, C40 launched the Cool Cities Accelerator with 33 founding cities. At COP30, the Beat the Heat initiative brought 185 cities together. ClimateWorks has a $50 million fund specifically for heat resilience work in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Sub-Saharan Africa.

What is missing is a scalable education layer. That is exactly where Globe From Home fits in.
Six proven, community-deployable interventions — each evidence-backed and structured in the Project Drawdown style.
Each shows: what it is · evidence of effectiveness · expected outcomes
🆘
Personal heat response & first aid
Training individuals to recognise heat illness, apply immediate cooling, identify vulnerable neighbours, activate buddy systems. Day 2 workshop.
Evidence
Ahmedabad HAP: ~1,190 lives saved/yr after community health worker training. Europe: up to 80% heat mortality reduction with structured adaptation (Nature Medicine 2024).
–31% heat illness (pilot target)50 people protected per champion1,625 cascade-trained per 500-champion cohort
🏚️
Cool roofs
Applying white/reflective coatings to rooftops. Reduces indoor temperatures and AC demand. Day 4 workshop.
Evidence
EPA Kansas City: 65,000 m² avoided 269 t CO₂/yr (~4.1 kg/m²). Indoor reduction: 2–5°C. Andalusia: 136,000 t CO₂/yr avoided at scale.
–2 to –5°C indoor2.5 kg CO₂/m²/yr (conservative)$12–45/tonne CO₂ at national scale
🌿
Urban gardens & green corridors
Planting trees and gardens to reduce the urban heat island effect. Day 6 workshop.
Evidence
Medellín: 30 green corridors → –2°C city-wide in 3 years at $6.50/person — most cost-effective urban cooling documented globally (WEF 2024).
–2.1°C ambient (pilot target)$6.50/person benchmark
🗺️
School heat mapping
Students map temperature hotspots across school buildings and grounds, producing a school resilience plan. Day 8 workshop.
Evidence
OECD 2026: 1°F hotter school year = –1% PSAT scores. Japan: 1 day above 34°C = –0.56 SD test scores. England/Paris mapped 670+ school grounds for targeted intervention.
+17% attendance (pilot target)Actionable plan per school
☀️
Solar-powered fans for schools
Grid-independent cooling for schools in low-income countries where AC is not feasible. Prioritises classrooms identified by heat mapping.
Evidence
Australia: AUD $477M invested to cool 649 state schools. Japan: universalised AC linked to maintained test scores. Solar fans = affordable, off-grid pathway.
+15% attendance (pilot target)No electricity cost
📣
Advocacy campaigns
Champions design and run localised heat awareness campaigns. Day 10 workshop. Each commits to cascade-training a minimum of 5 others.
Evidence
Barcelona: climate shelter network grew 70→368 shelters (2020–2024) via advocacy. Phoenix: 33,000+ cooling centre visits where outreach was active. Awareness drives uptake.
–28% heat symptoms (pilot target)600 people trained (programme target)×5 cascade per champion

Key research underpinning this programme
Community HATS — largest ever longitudinal evaluation of worker heat stress
Led by Balsari and Buckee at Harvard, funded by Wellcome ($3M) and the Salata Institute. Tracking 800+ women informal workers in India with wearable temperature sensors for 12+ months. Will generate 9.5 million distinct observations on heat exposure and its effects on health, wages, and wellbeing. The first dataset of this kind in the world — and it directly validates the Globe From Home community training approach. Harvard Chan School news →
What the research says about cool roofs — directly relevant to Day 4
Balsari's team found that affordable financing to paint roofs white was among the most effective and locally-demanded heat interventions among India's informal workers — cited in Harvard Gazette, May 2025. This is exactly what Globe From Home teaches in Day 4.
